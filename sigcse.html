SIGCSE

        “ When Are We Gonna Have a Serious Conversation About How Unserious (and Problematic) SIGCSE Is?” authored by Dr Nicki Washington, published by Momentum, is a short yet detailed response to criticism given to the author. Dr Washington walks the audience through a reflection of the response given back from reviewers at SIGCSE and to readers' surprise, much of the critic was rooted in their inability to understand the work proposed. The author further reflects on how specifically each reviewer spoke from a perspective that stemmed from an elitist, white and ignorant point of view. As well as touching on the fact that for some diversity, equity and inclusion is an act for them; it is something they do or not do when it conveniently benefits them.  

        Dr Nicki Washington’s main argument includes the Special Interest Group in CS Education may not have the right system or participants with an ability to accurately review every proposal submitted. Dr Washington uses specific and detailed examples with the use of quotes to illustrate how not every reviewer associated with SIGCSE is right to serve as a reviewer for every proposal. An example of this is seen when the author reflects on a comment left by a reviewer describing how due to the panelists not all being full time tenure employees, this may make it hard for people to evaluate the depth of their education on the topic. The author points out that to underestimate a person's ability to understand a topic based on their educational status stems from an elitist mindset and speaks to the reviewer's ability to review this proposal. Following, readers are then taken to another comment left by a reviewer that corrects Dr Washington’s work cited that corrects the spelling and capitalization of well known author bell hooks, who is known to never capitalize their name. Which leads readers to believe, this reviewer may not actually be equipped to review this proposal, if they do not know bell hooks work.

        Although SIGCSE has the right to review and reject proposals as their company sees fit, it does bring to question the company's overall goals of a forum, if volunteers apply and receive an overdone critic. It calls into question the company’s mission statement that reflects it being a global forum which insinuates diversity. But it also causes readers to reflect on their use of the word educators, since based on the reviews left they have a preconceived standard as to who is considered an educator. Most importantly, readers may want to think twice on attending discussions since the company’s underlying goal is to see where the discussions can provide profit to the company.
